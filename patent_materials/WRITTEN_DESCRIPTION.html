SYSTEM AND METHOD FOR CATEGORIZING, RANKING, AND DISTRIBUTING AI-POWERED COMMUNITY PLANS WITH PEER-TO-PEER SHARING, SOCIAL MEDIA REDISTRIBUTION, AND MONETIZATION STRATEGY

FIELD OF THE INVENTION

The present invention relates generally to artificial intelligence-powered planning and peer-to-peer content distribution systems. More particularly, the invention relates to systems and methods for generating, categorizing, ranking, monetizing, and distributing AI-generated community plans using location-based and real-time data, with mechanisms for peer-to-peer sharing, social media redistribution, verified expert content, community copying, editing, and ownership of plans based on user-defined budget controls, theme-based filtering criteria, and priority systems.

BACKGROUND OF THE INVENTION

Traditional planning systems and content distribution platforms suffer from several fundamental limitations. First, conventional planning tools require users to manually research, compile, and organize information from disparate sources, resulting in significant time investment and incomplete or outdated information. Users seeking to plan travel, events, or activities must consult multiple websites, manually verify current conditions, and cross-reference information across platforms.

Second, existing content sharing platforms lack intelligent categorization systems that prioritize content based on urgency, commercial value, expert verification, popularity trends, and community relevance. Content is typically presented chronologically or through simplistic popularity metrics that fail to account for time-sensitive information, sponsored content value, verified expert contributions, or emergency situations requiring immediate user attention.

Third, current systems do not effectively integrate location-based real-time data into AI-generated plans. Users receive static plans that quickly become outdated when weather conditions change, safety advisories are issued, or local events impact planned activities. The lack of dynamic data integration renders plans obsolete and potentially unsafe.

Fourth, conventional platforms lack sophisticated budget control, theme-based categorization, and filtering mechanisms that allow users to discover plans matching their financial constraints, thematic preferences, and personal requirements. Users must manually evaluate whether shared plans fit their budget or desired experience type, leading to wasted time reviewing irrelevant content.

Fifth, existing systems do not provide clear monetization pathways for plan creators, verified experts, platforms, or sponsors. The absence of integrated monetization mechanisms prevents sustainable business models for community planning platforms, limits opportunities for commercial entities to reach relevant audiences, and fails to incentivize expert contributors to share verified high-quality plans.

Sixth, prior art systems fail to establish clear ownership and editing rights when users copy community-shared plans in peer-to-peer fashion. Ambiguity regarding whether copied plans remain linked to originals versus becoming independent user-owned content creates confusion and limits user confidence in modifying shared plans.

Seventh, current systems lack comprehensive social media redistribution capabilities enabling users to share AI-generated plans across multiple social platforms with properly formatted previews, attribution tracking, privacy protection, and viral monetization opportunities. Users cannot easily distribute plans to external audiences via Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, or other social networks, limiting plan discovery and platform growth.

Eighth, existing platforms do not distinguish between user-generated content and expert-verified premium content. The absence of verification badges and associated quality certification processes prevents users from identifying professionally reviewed plans created or endorsed by tourism experts, destination authorities, or recognized organizations, reducing trust in shared content.

Therefore, there exists a need for an improved system and method that generates AI-powered plans enriched with location-based and real-time data, categorizes and ranks plans using multi-tier badge systems reflecting urgency, commercial sponsorship, expert verification, and community engagement, enables peer-to-peer sharing with clear ownership transfer, facilitates social media redistribution with privacy protection, implements theme-based and budget-controlled discovery and filtering, provides clear monetization mechanisms including revenue sharing with verified creators, and establishes unambiguous ownership and editing rights for community-shared plans.

SUMMARY OF THE INVENTION

The present invention addresses the aforementioned limitations by providing a comprehensive system and method for categorizing, ranking, and distributing AI-powered community plans with peer-to-peer sharing, social media redistribution, and integrated monetization strategies. The invention leverages artificial intelligence to generate personalized plans incorporating location-based and real-time data, presents plans through a hierarchical badge categorization system including verified expert content, enables peer-to-peer community sharing with clear ownership transfer, facilitates social media redistribution with privacy protection and viral tracking, implements theme-based and budget-controlled filtering and priority ranking mechanisms, and provides monetization pathways including creator revenue sharing.

In accordance with one embodiment, the invention comprises a computer-implemented method for generating and distributing AI-powered community plans via peer-to-peer sharing and social media channels. The method includes: receiving user input defining planning parameters including origin location, destination, duration, budget constraints, theme preferences, and occasion type; querying one or more artificial intelligence models to generate a structured plan comprising activities, tasks, scheduling, and resource allocation; enriching the generated plan with location-based real-time data including current weather conditions, safety advisories, traffic patterns, and local events; assigning a hierarchical badge category to the plan selected from a plurality of badge types including Emergency badges for urgent safety-critical information, Verified badges for expert-reviewed premium content, Sponsored badges for commercially promoted content, Trending badges for high-engagement popular content, and Community badges for user-generated non-commercial content; calculating a priority ranking score based on badge type, user engagement metrics, temporal relevance, theme matching, and location proximity; displaying the plan in a discovery interface ordered by priority ranking score and filtered by theme categories; enabling peer-to-peer plan copying whereby upon user selection to copy a displayed plan, creating a new user-owned instance of the plan with full editing rights independent of the original plan; and facilitating social media redistribution whereby users can share plans to external social platforms with formatted previews, privacy protection redacting personally identifiable information, attribution tracking, and optional affiliate monetization links.

The hierarchical badge system establishes a clear priority order wherein Emergency badges receive highest priority for time-critical safety information such as natural disasters, travel advisories, or urgent public health alerts. Verified badges receive second-tier priority, designating expert-reviewed plans created or endorsed by tourism professionals, destination authorities, government agencies, or recognized organizations, with associated revenue sharing mechanisms rewarding verified content creators. Sponsored badges receive third-tier priority, enabling commercial entities to promote relevant plans while generating platform revenue. Trending badges identify organically popular community content based on engagement metrics including view counts, copy rates, and completion statistics. Community badges designate standard user-generated content without emergency status, expert verification, commercial sponsorship, or trending popularity.

The invention includes theme-based categorization and filtering enabling users to discover plans matching specific experience types. Theme categories include but are not limited to: adventure travel emphasizing outdoor activities and exploration; luxury experiences featuring premium accommodations and services; budget-conscious plans optimizing cost efficiency; family-friendly content suitable for children and multi-generational groups; foodie and culinary tourism focusing on dining experiences; cultural and historical exploration; wellness and relaxation retreats; outdoor and nature-based activities; romantic and honeymoon experiences; business and professional travel; and custom user-defined themes. Theme-based filtering operates in conjunction with budget controls, location preferences, and duration filters to enable highly targeted plan discovery.

The invention further includes sophisticated budget control mechanisms enabling users to filter discoverable plans based on estimated total costs. Each plan includes budget metadata calculated by summing projected costs for activities, accommodations, transportation, and other resource expenditures. Users specify maximum budget thresholds, and the system filters displayed plans to show only those meeting budget criteria, preventing wasted time reviewing financially infeasible options.

The real-time data integration component continuously queries external data sources including weather services, government safety advisory systems, traffic monitoring systems, and event calendars. When conditions change affecting a plan's viability or safety, the system updates plan metadata, adjusts priority rankings, and in critical cases, promotes plans to Emergency badge status with prominent visual indicators alerting users to changed conditions.

The peer-to-peer ownership and editing model establishes that when a user copies a community-shared plan, the copied plan becomes a new independent entity fully owned by the copying user with unrestricted editing rights. Modifications to copied plans do not affect original plans, and original plan creators have no visibility into or control over copied plan modifications. This clear ownership transfer model encourages community sharing by eliminating concerns about unauthorized modifications to original content while empowering users to freely customize copied plans.

The social media redistribution component enables users to share plans across multiple external platforms including Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok, and others. The system generates platform-specific formatted previews including plan images, titles, descriptions, budget summaries, and badge indicators optimized for each platform's requirements. Privacy protection mechanisms automatically redact personally identifiable information and sensitive details before generating shareable content. Attribution tracking monitors viral distribution by assigning unique identifiers to shared links and tracking subsequent plan adoptions originating from social shares. Optional monetization features enable injection of affiliate links or sponsored content references into shared plans, creating viral revenue opportunities.

The verified content and creator monetization system establishes processes for expert review and certification of premium plans. Tourism professionals, destination marketing organizations, government tourism agencies, travel industry experts, and recognized organizations can submit plans for verification or create verified plans directly. Verification processes include quality assessment, accuracy verification of information, compliance with destination regulations, and safety validation. Verified badge assignment confers visual distinction via checkmark indicators and distinctive coloring, elevated priority in discovery rankings, and eligibility for creator revenue sharing wherein verified content creators receive portions of platform revenue or direct user payments for premium verified plan access.

The monetization strategy enables multiple revenue streams including: sponsored plan placement fees from commercial entities seeking to promote plans to targeted audiences; subscription tiers providing enhanced features such as unlimited plan generation, advanced filtering, plan analytics, and access to verified premium content; revenue sharing with verified content creators based on plan adoption metrics and premium access fees; and viral affiliate monetization from social media shares generating platform revenue when shared plans drive transactions. The badge system's explicit designation of Sponsored and Verified content maintains transparency while enabling sustainable platform economics and incentivizing quality content creation.

DETAILED DESCRIPTION OF THE INVENTION

System Architecture Overview

Referring to FIG. 1, the invention comprises several interconnected modules operating in coordination to deliver the complete functionality. The AI Planning Engine 100 receives user inputs and generates structured plans. The Real-Time Data Enrichment Module 200 queries external data sources and integrates current information into plans. The Badge Categorization and Ranking Engine 300 assigns badge types and calculates priority scores, including processing of verified expert content. The Monetization Module 400 manages sponsored placements, subscription revenue processing, and creator revenue sharing. The Discovery Interface 500 presents plans to users with theme-based filtering, budget controls, and sorting capabilities. The Peer-to-Peer Community Sharing Module 600 handles plan copying, ownership transfer, and editing rights management. The Social Media Redistribution Module 700 generates platform-specific shareable content with privacy protection and viral tracking.

AI Planning Engine

The AI Planning Engine 100 interfaces with one or more large language model APIs to generate comprehensive plans based on user-provided parameters. In one embodiment, the engine queries OpenAI's GPT-4 model, Anthropic's Claude model, or other comparable AI systems. The engine implements a multi-turn conversation flow that progressively gathers planning requirements through structured questions.

For rapid planning scenarios, the engine employs a Quick Plan mode requesting five essential parameters: origin location, destination location, duration, budget range, and occasion type. The AI model processes these inputs and generates a preliminary plan structure including recommended activities, time allocations, and resource requirements.

For comprehensive planning scenarios, the engine employs a Smart Plan mode gathering up to ten parameters through a three-batch question flow. Additional parameters may include theme preferences (adventure, luxury, budget, family, foodie, cultural, wellness, outdoor, romantic, business, or custom themes), accommodation preferences, dietary restrictions, mobility considerations, group size, and activity intensity preferences. The expanded parameter set enables generation of highly personalized plans matching specific user requirements and thematic preferences.

The engine includes validation logic preventing accidental plan completion before users provide sufficient information. The system detects override keywords such as "create plan now" allowing users to bypass remaining questions, while preventing ambiguous inputs from prematurely triggering plan generation.

Generated plans conform to a standardized data structure including: plan title, description, total estimated budget with itemized cost breakdowns, theme category assignments, activity list with individual activities containing descriptions, estimated costs, duration, and location coordinates, task checklist items, scheduling information, and metadata enabling theme-based and budget-based filtering. This structured format enables consistent processing by downstream modules.

Real-Time Data Enrichment Module

The Real-Time Data Enrichment Module 200 enhances AI-generated plans with current information from external data sources. The module queries weather APIs to retrieve forecasts for destination locations during planned travel dates. Weather data includes temperature ranges, precipitation probability, severe weather warnings, and seasonal condition assessments.

The module queries government travel advisory systems and safety databases to identify relevant warnings, travel restrictions, natural disasters, political unrest, health advisories, and other safety-critical information affecting destination locations. When critical safety issues are detected, the module flags plans for potential Emergency badge assignment.

Traffic and transportation data sources provide current travel time estimates, road closures, public transit disruptions, and transportation cost updates. This information ensures plan timing remains realistic and identifies potential logistical challenges.

Local event calendars and tourism databases supply information about festivals, concerts, sporting events, seasonal attractions, and temporary closures that may impact planned activities. Event data enables the AI Planning Engine to suggest relevant activities and avoid scheduling conflicts.

The enrichment process occurs both at initial plan generation and through periodic background updates for saved plans. When real-time data indicates significant changes affecting plan viability, the system notifies plan owners and updates plan status accordingly.

Badge Categorization and Ranking Engine

The Badge Categorization and Ranking Engine 300 assigns hierarchical badge designations to plans and calculates priority ranking scores determining display order in the Discovery Interface. Referring to FIG. 2, the badge system implements five distinct categories with descending priority: Emergency, Verified, Sponsored, Trending, and Community.

Emergency badges are assigned to plans containing urgent safety-critical information requiring immediate user attention. Assignment criteria include: detection of active natural disasters affecting plan locations such as hurricanes, earthquakes, floods, or wildfires; government-issued travel warnings or restrictions; public health emergencies; or other time-sensitive safety concerns. Emergency badged plans receive maximum priority scores ensuring prominent display positioning. Visual presentation includes distinctive red coloring and warning iconography alerting users to urgency.

Verified badges are assigned to plans that have undergone expert review and certification processes. Verification may be conducted by tourism professionals, destination marketing organizations, government tourism agencies, travel industry experts with recognized credentials, or reputable organizations with relevant expertise. The verification process includes quality assessment evaluating plan accuracy, completeness, and utility; factual verification confirming activity availability, pricing accuracy, and logistical feasibility; compliance checking ensuring adherence to local regulations, safety standards, and ethical tourism practices; and safety validation confirming plans do not recommend unsafe activities or compromised locations. Verified plans receive second-tier priority scores below Emergency but above Sponsored badges. Visual presentation includes distinctive checkmark indicators, special coloring (e.g., blue or green), and "Verified by [Expert/Organization Name]" attribution. Verified badge assignment qualifies plans for creator revenue sharing programs wherein verified content creators receive portions of platform subscription revenue, direct premium access fees from users accessing verified-only content, or performance-based payments tied to plan adoption metrics.

Sponsored badges are assigned to plans for which commercial entities have paid placement fees. The Monetization Module 400 processes sponsor payments and marks eligible plans for Sponsored badge assignment. Sponsored plans receive third-tier priority scores below Emergency and Verified but above Trending and Community badges. Visual presentation includes distinctive purple coloring and sponsor designation clearly identifying commercial content while maintaining professional appearance. Sponsored badge placement enables sustainable platform revenue while providing value to commercial entities seeking to reach relevant audiences.

Trending badges are assigned to organically popular community plans based on engagement metrics. The engine calculates engagement scores by aggregating: view counts weighted by recency, copy/adoption rates, task completion rates from users who adopted the plan, user ratings or feedback when available, and social media share counts when plans are redistributed via external platforms. Plans exceeding dynamically adjusted trending thresholds receive Trending badge assignments. Trending badges employ gold coloring signifying popular high-quality content. Trending status creates positive feedback loops where popular plans receive enhanced visibility, encouraging quality content creation.

Community badges represent the baseline category for user-generated plans without Emergency status, expert verification, commercial sponsorship, or trending popularity. Community badges employ neutral gray coloring and standard visual presentation. While Community badged plans receive lower priority scores than other categories, high-quality Community plans can achieve Trending status through organic engagement, and outstanding community contributors may be invited to become verified creators, providing upward mobility within the badge hierarchy.

The priority ranking algorithm combines badge type with additional factors including: temporal relevance scoring plans matching current seasons or upcoming dates higher; location proximity scoring plans near user's current location or stated interests higher; theme matching scoring plans aligning with user's specified theme preferences higher; user preference matching based on historical plan adoptions and expressed interests; and recency factors preventing stale content from dominating results. The weighted combination of these factors produces numerical priority scores used for sorting plans in the Discovery Interface.

Theme-Based Categorization and Discovery

The invention implements comprehensive theme-based categorization enabling users to discover plans matching specific experience types, activity preferences, and travel styles. Theme categories are assigned during plan generation based on AI analysis of planned activities, accommodation types, budget levels, and user-specified preferences. Multiple theme tags may be assigned to a single plan when content spans multiple categories.

Adventure theme encompasses plans featuring outdoor exploration, physical activities, and novel experiences including hiking, climbing, water sports, wildlife encounters, expedition travel, and adventure sports. Luxury theme designates plans emphasizing premium accommodations, fine dining, exclusive experiences, private services, and high-end amenities. Budget theme identifies cost-optimized plans maximizing value while minimizing expenditures through economical accommodation, free or low-cost activities, and efficient transportation.

Family-friendly theme indicates plans suitable for children and multi-generational groups, featuring child-appropriate activities, family accommodations, and logistical considerations for traveling with minors. Foodie and culinary theme highlights plans centered on dining experiences, cooking classes, food tours, wine tasting, and gastronomic exploration. Cultural and historical theme encompasses plans focused on museums, historical sites, cultural events, traditional experiences, and educational activities.

Wellness and relaxation theme designates plans featuring spa treatments, meditation retreats, yoga, health-focused activities, and stress reduction experiences. Outdoor and nature theme emphasizes natural environments, parks, beaches, forests, scenic landscapes, and nature-based activities. Romantic and honeymoon theme identifies plans designed for couples seeking intimate experiences, romantic settings, and special occasions.

Business and professional travel theme encompasses plans optimized for work travel including conference attendance, meeting logistics, efficient scheduling, and business-appropriate accommodations. Custom theme tags enable users and verified creators to define specialized categories such as solo travel, backpacking, photography-focused trips, accessibility-optimized plans, sustainable eco-tourism, or any other specialized focus.

Theme-based filtering in the Discovery Interface allows users to select one or multiple theme categories, displaying only plans tagged with matching themes. Theme filters operate in conjunction with budget controls, location preferences, duration filters, and badge type filters to enable highly targeted plan discovery matching specific user requirements.

Monetization Module

The Monetization Module 400 implements revenue generation mechanisms supporting sustainable platform operations and incentivizing quality content creation. The module manages multiple revenue streams tailored to different stakeholder needs.

Sponsored plan placement fees enable commercial entities including tourism boards, hospitality providers, event organizers, travel companies, and destination marketing organizations to promote relevant plans to targeted audiences. The module provides sponsor account management, payment processing via integration with payment processors such as Stripe, placement budget configuration, audience targeting parameters (geographic regions, budget ranges, theme categories, demographic factors), and performance analytics showing impression counts, click-through rates, adoption metrics, and return on advertising spend. Sponsors create or designate existing plans for promotion, configure target audience parameters, and fund placement campaigns. The module calculates cost-per-impression or cost-per-action billing and processes payments through secure payment gateways.

Subscription tier monetization offers tiered service levels to end users. A free tier provides limited monthly plan generations (e.g., 5 plans per month) with unlimited plan browsing, real-time data access, and access to Community, Trending, and Sponsored plans. A Pro tier priced at monthly subscription rates (e.g., $6.99/month) provides unlimited plan generation, access to Verified premium content, advanced filtering options including theme-based discovery, plan export capabilities, analytics showing personal plan performance, and priority customer support. A Family tier priced at higher monthly rates (e.g., $14.99/month) extends Pro benefits to multiple user accounts (e.g., up to 5 users) and adds collaborative planning features enabling family members to jointly create and edit plans. The module tracks usage limits, processes recurring subscription payments, manages trial periods (e.g., 7-day free trials for Pro and Family tiers), and enforces tier-appropriate feature access.

Creator revenue sharing provides monetization opportunities for verified content creators. Verified contributors receive compensation through multiple mechanisms: subscription revenue sharing wherein a percentage of Pro and Family tier subscription fees is distributed to verified creators based on how frequently their verified plans are viewed, copied, or completed by paying subscribers; premium content access fees where users pay one-time fees or microtransactions to access specific high-value verified plans, with creators receiving majority revenue shares; performance bonuses rewarding top-performing verified creators based on engagement metrics, user satisfaction ratings, and plan completion rates; and sponsored content commissions wherein verified creators receive additional compensation when sponsors choose to promote their verified plans, creating aligned incentives for quality commercial content.

Viral affiliate monetization enables revenue generation from social media redistribution. When users share plans to external platforms, the system optionally injects affiliate links or tracked referral codes into shared content. Subsequent transactions originating from social shares (e.g., users clicking affiliate links to book accommodations, activities, or services referenced in shared plans) generate commission revenue split between the platform, the plan creator, and the sharing user. This creates viral growth incentives while monetizing social distribution channels.

The module implements usage tracking counters monitoring plan generation counts against tier limits. When free tier users approach or exceed limits, the system presents upgrade prompts encouraging subscription conversion and highlighting benefits of Pro or Family tiers including unlimited plan generation and verified content access. Payment processing integration handles secure credit card processing, subscription renewals, cancellations, refund processing following platform policies, and creator payout disbursements.

Discovery Interface and Filtering

The Discovery Interface 500, illustrated in FIG. 2, presents community plans to users with comprehensive filtering, sorting, and search capabilities optimized for theme-based and budget-controlled discovery. The interface displays plans as visual cards containing: plan title, creator attribution with verification status if applicable, destination location, estimated budget with cost breakdown, badge designation with appropriate visual styling (Emergency red, Verified checkmark, Sponsored purple, Trending gold, Community gray), theme category tags, preview image representative of destination or activity, brief description, and engagement metrics such as adoption counts and user ratings.

Budget filtering enables users to specify maximum total budget thresholds. The interface includes slider controls or numeric input fields for setting budget limits with currency selection supporting multiple currencies. Upon budget filter application, the system queries the plan database for plans with total estimated costs at or below the specified threshold, displaying only budget-appropriate results. Budget range filtering also enables minimum budget specification, allowing users to filter for premium experiences exceeding certain cost thresholds. This filtering prevents users from reviewing plans financially out of reach or insufficiently luxurious for their preferences, significantly improving discovery efficiency.

Theme-based filtering enables users to select one or multiple theme categories from the comprehensive theme taxonomy. Users select desired themes via checkbox controls, dropdown menus, or visual theme icons, and the system filters results to show only plans tagged with matching themes. Multi-theme selection supports Boolean logic wherein users can specify "AND" logic (plans must match all selected themes) or "OR" logic (plans matching any selected theme are displayed), enabling precise discovery control.

Category filtering enables users to narrow results by plan type categories such as travel plans, event plans, project plans, or custom categories. Users select desired categories via checkbox or dropdown controls, and the system filters results accordingly.

Location filtering allows users to specify geographic regions, countries, states, or cities of interest. The system filters plans based on primary destination locations, showing only geographically relevant results. Location proximity filtering additionally enables showing plans within specified distances from user's current location or specified reference points.

Duration filtering enables users to specify trip or project length preferences such as weekend trips (1-3 days), week-long experiences (4-7 days), extended vacations (8-14 days), or multi-week expeditions (15+ days). The system filters plans matching duration criteria, with customizable duration range specifications.

Badge type filtering allows users to filter by specific badge categories, for example showing only Verified plans to access expert-reviewed content, only Emergency plans to view safety-critical information, or excluding Sponsored plans to see only organic community content. This enables users to customize discovery based on trust preferences and content source requirements.

Sorting controls allow users to reorder results by different criteria including: priority score (default), showing highest-priority Emergency, Verified, and Sponsored plans first; budget (low to high or high to low); popularity based on adoption counts and engagement metrics; recency showing newest plans first; user rating showing highest-rated plans first; or theme relevance showing best theme matches first. Sort order preference enables users to prioritize different discovery factors based on current needs.

Search functionality enables free-text keyword searching across plan titles, descriptions, locations, activity content, and theme tags. The system executes database queries matching search terms and displays relevant results, with optional search highlighting showing matched terms and relevance scoring ranking search results by match quality.

Peer-to-Peer Community Sharing and Ownership Transfer

The Peer-to-Peer Community Sharing Module 600 implements decentralized mechanisms for users to share created plans directly with the community and for other users to copy, own, and edit shared plans in peer-to-peer fashion. The module establishes clear ownership and editing rights preventing ambiguity while enabling viral plan distribution through community networks.

When a user completes plan generation via the AI Planning Engine, the system presents options to save the plan as private (visible only to the creating user) or share with the community (visible in Discovery Interface and accessible to all platform users). Users selecting community sharing consent to making the plan publicly discoverable while retaining ownership of the original plan instance. Sharing users may optionally configure their plan as eligible for verified status if they possess relevant expertise or organizational credentials, triggering verification review processes.

Community-shared plans appear in the Discovery Interface subject to badge categorization, theme-based filtering, and priority ranking as previously described. Users browsing the Discovery Interface can view plan details and select "Copy Plan", "Use This Plan", or "Adopt Plan" actions, all of which trigger the peer-to-peer ownership transfer process.

Upon copy action, the Peer-to-Peer Community Sharing Module creates a new plan database record containing all data from the original plan including activities, tasks, budget information, locations, and theme tags. Critically, the new plan record establishes the copying user as the owner, with no linkage to the original plan beyond initial data copying and optional attribution metadata. The copied plan becomes an independent entity with its own database identifier, edit history, access controls, and future sharing permissions. This peer-to-peer transfer model contrasts with centralized platforms maintaining continuous linkages between original and derived content.

The copying user receives full editing rights over the copied plan, including: ability to add, remove, or modify activities; adjust scheduling and time allocations; update budget estimates; add or remove tasks; change plan titles and descriptions; modify theme category assignments; adjust privacy settings; and mark tasks as completed. Editing operations modify only the copied plan instance, with zero effect on the original shared plan. The peer-to-peer architecture ensures plan copies function as fully independent user-owned assets.

This ownership transfer model contrasts with reference-based systems where copied content maintains links to originals. By creating fully independent copies via peer-to-peer transfer, the invention eliminates several problems: original plan creators cannot see modifications to copied plans, preventing privacy concerns when users personalize plans with sensitive information; changes to original plans do not propagate to copies, preventing unexpected modifications to user-customized plans; users can freely modify copied plans without concerns about affecting other users or violating original creator intentions; and the system avoids complex synchronization logic managing relationships between originals and copies. The peer-to-peer model maximizes user autonomy and ownership while enabling viral content distribution.

The module implements duplicate detection logic identifying when users attempt to copy plans they previously copied. Upon detecting duplicate copy attempts, the system presents confirmation dialogs offering to update the user's existing copy with any changes from the original plan or create a new independent copy for comparison purposes. This prevents unintended proliferation of identical plan copies in user accounts while supporting use cases where users intentionally want multiple variations of similar plans.

Peer-based recommendation algorithms analyze user copying patterns, plan modification behaviors, and completion metrics to identify plans likely to interest specific users based on plans previously copied or adopted by users with similar preferences. This peer-to-peer discovery mechanism surfaces relevant content through collaborative filtering rather than centralized editorial curation.

The module optionally tracks attribution metadata recording plan lineage when users choose to credit original creators. Attribution is voluntary and user-controlled, enabling users to publicly acknowledge plan sources or maintain private copying for personal use. When plans are shared to social media via the Social Media Redistribution Module, users can choose whether to include creator attribution in shared previews.

Social Media Redistribution Module

The Social Media Redistribution Module 700 enables users to share AI-generated plans across external social media platforms with platform-optimized formatting, privacy protection, viral tracking, and optional monetization. The module generates shareable content for multiple platforms including Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, TikTok, Pinterest, WhatsApp, and others.

When users select social sharing options, the module generates platform-specific formatted previews optimized for each platform's technical requirements and user expectations. Instagram previews include visually striking square or vertical images featuring plan destination photos, overlay text displaying plan title and budget summary, badge indicators showing Emergency, Verified, Sponsored, or Trending status, and call-to-action text encouraging followers to view full plan details. Facebook previews include Open Graph metadata with plan titles, descriptions, images, and direct links to plan detail pages, enabling rich link previews in Facebook feeds. Twitter previews include Twitter Card metadata with concise plan summaries fitting character limits, relevant hashtags based on theme categories and destinations, and shortened tracking links. LinkedIn previews emphasize professional travel and business trip plans with appropriate formatting for professional audiences.

The module implements comprehensive privacy protection mechanisms before generating shareable content. Privacy Shield functionality automatically scans plan content for personally identifiable information (PII) including names, addresses, phone numbers, email addresses, and financial information; protected health information (PHI) including medical conditions, medications, and health-related details; and other sensitive information users may have added to private notes or customizations. Detected sensitive information is automatically redacted from shared previews, replaced with generic placeholders, or excluded entirely from social media versions. Users receive preview screens showing exactly what will be shared, with manual override options to include or exclude specific content elements, ensuring informed consent before social distribution.

Attribution tracking assigns unique identifiers to each social share, encoding user ID, plan ID, platform, and timestamp into shareable link parameters. When external users click shared links and subsequently view plan details, copy plans, create accounts, or subscribe to paid tiers, the system records the social share source enabling viral growth measurement and attribution. Attribution data feeds into engagement metrics influencing Trending badge assignments and creator revenue sharing calculations.

Viral monetization features optionally inject affiliate links or tracked referral codes into socially shared plans. When plans reference specific hotels, restaurants, activities, or services with available affiliate programs, the module includes affiliate links in shared content. Users clicking shared plans and subsequently booking referenced services generate commission revenue. Revenue sharing distributes affiliate earnings among the platform, original plan creator (if applicable), and user who shared to social media, creating aligned incentives for high-quality viral distribution. Users control whether their social shares include affiliate monetization, with options to share "clean" non-commercial versions or enable monetization to earn referral commissions.

The module tracks viral propagation by monitoring when shared plans generate secondary shares, creating viral chain tracking. Analytics dashboards show users which of their shared plans achieved viral reach, which platforms drove highest engagement, and cumulative reach metrics. High-performing social sharers may qualify for creator program invitations or platform partnership opportunities.

Cross-platform scheduling features enable users to schedule social shares for optimal posting times across multiple platforms simultaneously, with platform-specific content variations. Integration with social media APIs enables direct posting where platform policies permit, or generates copy-paste ready content with formatted text, hashtags, and images when direct API posting is unavailable.

Priority System Implementation

The priority system integrates badge hierarchy with additional ranking factors to optimize plan discovery across themes, budgets, and user preferences. The system assigns base priority scores according to badge type: Emergency badges receive base scores in the range 9000-10000; Verified badges receive base scores in the range 8000-8999; Sponsored badges receive base scores in the range 7000-7999; Trending badges receive base scores in the range 5000-6999; and Community badges receive base scores in the range 1000-4999.

Modifier calculations adjust base scores based on supplementary factors. Temporal relevance modifiers increase scores for plans matching current or upcoming seasons, with higher boosts for plans optimally timed for immediate use. Location proximity modifiers increase scores for plans near user's current location or stated destination interests. Theme matching modifiers significantly boost scores for plans matching user's selected theme filters or historically preferred themes. Engagement velocity modifiers increase Trending badge scores for plans showing accelerating adoption rates indicating emerging popularity. Recency modifiers provide slight boosts to newer plans preventing excessive dominance by older established content while still rewarding proven quality. Budget alignment modifiers boost plans closely matching user's specified budget targets. Verification recency modifiers ensure recently verified content receives priority over older verified plans that may require re-verification.

The weighted combination of base scores and modifiers produces final priority scores used for sorting Discovery Interface results. The algorithm ensures Emergency content always appears first regardless of other factors, followed by Verified content, Sponsored content, Trending content, and finally Community content, while allowing high-performing content within each tier to outrank lower-performing content within the same tier based on theme matching, budget alignment, and engagement metrics.

Verified Content Creator Program

The Verified Content Creator Program establishes processes and incentives for expert contributors to create and share high-quality verified plans. The program includes application processes for individuals and organizations seeking verified creator status, verification workflows for reviewing submitted plans, quality standards and guidelines for verified content, and revenue sharing mechanisms rewarding verified contributions.

Prospective verified creators apply through application forms providing credentials, expertise documentation, organizational affiliations, and sample plans demonstrating quality. Applications are reviewed by platform administrators or automated credential verification systems checking professional certifications, tourism industry affiliations, organizational legitimacy, and content quality. Approved applicants receive verified creator accounts with enhanced privileges including direct verified badge assignment abilities (subject to quality review), access to creator analytics dashboards, priority customer support, and revenue sharing enrollment.

Verification workflows for plan review include automated quality checks examining plan completeness, factual consistency, and formatting compliance; manual expert review by platform staff or peer verified creators assessing accuracy, safety, and utility; destination-specific validation confirming current availability of referenced activities, accurate pricing, and compliance with local regulations; and user feedback integration monitoring how verified plans perform with actual users, with ongoing quality enforcement removing verified status from underperforming or outdated content.

Quality standards require verified plans to include: accurate budgets with itemized cost breakdowns; realistic scheduling and time allocations; safety information and appropriate warnings; accessibility information when relevant; current information confirmed within specified time periods; high-quality images and descriptions; comprehensive activity details; and clear theme category assignments. Verified creators must maintain quality standards to preserve verified status, with periodic content audits ensuring ongoing accuracy.

Revenue sharing mechanisms distribute platform revenue to verified creators through multiple channels as previously described in the Monetization Module section. Creator earnings dashboards provide transparency showing revenue attribution to specific verified plans, engagement metrics driving revenue calculations, and payment schedules. Top-performing verified creators may receive featured placement, marketing support, and partnership opportunities with tourism organizations and sponsors.

Data Privacy and User Control

The system implements comprehensive data privacy protections respecting user confidentiality while enabling peer-to-peer sharing and social media redistribution. User-generated plans marked as private remain inaccessible to other users and excluded from Discovery Interface results. Community-shared plans display creator attribution but do not expose personally identifiable information unless users explicitly include such information in plan content.

Privacy Shield technology automatically detects and redacts sensitive information from plans before social media sharing, preventing unintentional disclosure of personal details. Users retain control over redaction decisions via preview screens showing exactly what will be shared publicly.

Users retain control over their shared plans via management interfaces enabling conversion of community-shared plans back to private status, removing them from Discovery Interface results and preventing future copying. Users can delete their created plans, permanently removing them from the system. However, previously copied instances of deleted plans remain in copying users' accounts per the peer-to-peer ownership transfer model, ensuring copied content remains available to users who adopted plans while respecting deletion requests from original creators.

Social media share attribution data is anonymized for privacy protection, with individual user identities protected while enabling aggregate viral tracking analytics. Users can opt out of viral tracking and affiliate monetization, choosing to share plans without tracking links or commercial elements.

Technical Implementation Details

In one embodiment, the system operates as a web-based application accessible via standard web browsers and mobile applications supporting iOS and Android platforms. The frontend interface implements responsive design supporting mobile devices, tablets, and desktop computers with theme-based visual styling and accessibility compliance. The backend comprises Node.js/Express server applications interfacing with PostgreSQL relational databases storing plan data, user accounts, badges, theme category assignments, engagement metrics, verification records, creator revenue data, social share tracking, and transaction records.

API integrations connect the system to external services including: OpenAI and Anthropic AI model APIs for plan generation; Tavily API for real-time web search and data enrichment; weather service APIs such as OpenWeatherMap or Weather.gov; government travel advisory APIs; traffic and transportation APIs such as Google Maps or similar services; event calendar and tourism databases; payment processing APIs such as Stripe for monetization functions and creator payouts; social media platform APIs for Instagram, Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn enabling direct posting and analytics; affiliate network APIs connecting to hotel, activity, and service booking platforms; and email service APIs for user notifications and creator communications.

The database schema includes tables for users, plans, activities, tasks, badges, theme categories, engagement metrics, subscriptions, sponsored placements, verified creator accounts, verification reviews, revenue transactions, social shares, viral tracking data, and attribution records. Foreign key relationships maintain referential integrity while the peer-to-peer ownership transfer model intentionally avoids creating references from copied plans back to original plans, ensuring true independence of copied content.

Caching mechanisms improve performance by temporarily storing API responses from external data sources, reducing redundant API calls and improving response times. Cache invalidation logic ensures data freshness by expiring cached weather, traffic, and safety data after appropriate time periods. Theme-based query results are cached with invalidation upon new plan additions or badge changes affecting theme-filtered discovery.

The system implements authentication and authorization controls verifying user identities via OAuth providers such as Google, Facebook, Apple, and email-based accounts. Authorization logic enforces access controls ensuring users can only edit plans they own, cannot access other users' private plans, and verified creator privileges are restricted to credentialed accounts. Subscription tier enforcement limits feature access based on user subscription status.

Social media integration employs OAuth authentication for platforms supporting API access, enabling users to connect social accounts for direct posting. Platform-specific preview generation handles varying image dimension requirements, character limits, metadata schemas, and embed capabilities across different social platforms. Link shortening services create trackable URLs encoding attribution data while maintaining clean aesthetics for social sharing.
